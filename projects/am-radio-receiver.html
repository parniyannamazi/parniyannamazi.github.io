AM Radio Receiver Design

Project Overview

Built and tested a functional AM radio receiver on a breadboard, implementing an LC tuner, diode-based envelope detector, and op-amp audio amplifier to tune and demodulate broadcast signals in the 550–1600 kHz band.

Skills Used

Analog Circuit Design Breadboarding LC Tuned Circuits Envelope Detection Op-Amp Amplifiers Oscilloscope

How It Works

An LC tuned circuit selects a station by resonating at its carrier frequency, picking it out of the 550–1600 kHz AM band. A diode-based envelope detector then strips away the carrier and recovers the audio signal riding on it. Finally an op-amp audio amplifier boosts that weak signal enough to drive headphones, so you can hear the demodulated broadcast.

Main Features

  • LC tuner that resonates at the selected station's carrier frequency to isolate it from the rest of the band.
  • Diode envelope detector that demodulates the AM signal and recovers the audio.
  • Op-amp audio amplifier stage to drive headphones.
  • Validated performance with an oscilloscope and by ear, adjusting component values to optimize resonance, bandwidth, detector ripple, and audio clarity.
