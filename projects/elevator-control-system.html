Elevator Control System on FPGA

Project Overview

Designed and implemented a complete digital elevator controller on a DE10-Standard FPGA using VHDL, Quartus, and ModelSim. Simulates a six-floor elevator with request latching, directional scheduling, timed movement, door operation, and an emergency stop.

Skills Used

VHDL FPGA Design Quartus ModelSim Digital Logic Design DE10-Standard

How It Works

Floor requests are entered with switches SW0–SW5 and confirmed with a push button. A request latch stores them until the elevator reaches that floor and finishes its door cycle. The scheduler then decides the next target: when moving up it serves all requests above the current floor first, when moving down it serves those below first, and when idle it picks the nearest request. The controller FSM drives the whole sequence — moving one floor at a time on a 1 Hz clock, opening the door on arrival, then returning to idle or serving the next request. Throughout, HEX0 shows the current floor, HEX1 shows the direction (or idle), and the LEDs light to show the door open at the served floor.

Main Features

  • Modular VHDL design split into clock divider, request latch, scheduler, timer, controller FSM, and display driver, connected in a top-level module.
  • Direction-based scheduler that serves requests in the current direction of travel first, and picks the nearest requested floor when idle.
  • Moves one floor at a time on a 1 Hz clock, showing the current floor on HEX0, direction/idle state on HEX1, and door status on the LEDs.
  • Emergency stop (SW9) that freezes all motion and door activity until cleared.
