Custom ESP32 PCB Design

Project Overview

Designed a 4-layer ESP32 development board with USB-C power input, onboard voltage regulation, and peripheral expansion headers — capturing the schematic and completing the full PCB layout in Altium.

Skills Used

Altium Designer PCB Design Schematic Capture 4-Layer Board USB-C DFM

How It Works

The board takes power in over USB-C, regulates it down to the voltages the ESP32 and peripherals need, and breaks the microcontroller's pins out to expansion headers. The design started as a schematic in Altium, which was then laid out as a 4-layer PCB — resolving design-rule-check (DRC) errors and applying clearance rules along the way, then finishing with manufacturability checks so it's ready to fabricate.

Main Features

  • 4-layer ESP32 development board with USB-C power input and onboard voltage regulation.
  • Peripheral expansion headers breaking out the ESP32's pins.
  • Full schematic capture and PCB layout in Altium, resolving DRC errors and applying clearance rules.
  • IPC-compliant footprints and 3D models, integrated into schematic and layout and finalized with manufacturability checks.
